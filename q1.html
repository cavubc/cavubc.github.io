Question:

What specific data is needed to enable the mapping of logical to physical IDs for IO Card hardware components?

Answer:

To enable effective mapping between logical and physical IDs for IO Card hardware components, we need to gather and manage the following data:

Logical IDs:

  • Device Names: The names assigned by the operating system or software (e.g., eth0, eth1).
  • Driver-Assigned IDs: Identifiers assigned by device drivers.
  • System-Assigned Identifiers: Unique IDs used within the management software.

Physical IDs:

  • Serial Numbers: Unique identifiers etched on the hardware.
  • Part Numbers: Manufacturer-assigned numbers indicating the component model.
  • Slot Numbers/Locations: The physical slot or port where the IO card is installed.
  • MAC Addresses: For network cards, the hardware addresses.

Mapping Information:

  • A dynamic database or table that correlates each logical ID with its physical counterpart.
  • Firmware and BIOS Data: Information from the system firmware that can aid in mapping.

Metadata:

  • Manufacturer Details: Vendor information for the IO cards.
  • Firmware Versions: Current firmware levels to aid in compatibility and updates.
  • Operational Status: Current health and performance metrics.
