Submitting a Feature Request in Rhythmic and Jira

To submit a feature request within Dell, follow the steps below to move from idea generation in Rhythmic to implementation in Jira. This ensures requests are organized, actionable, and tracked effectively.

1. Start in Rhythmic

  • Create a New Idea: In Rhythmic, navigate to the Ideas section and create a new entry. Give it a descriptive title that clearly identifies the feature.
  • Detail the Feature: Add a specific description, acting as an “elevator pitch.” Explain what the feature is, why it’s needed, and how it aligns with current objectives.
  • Justify the Need: Provide a brief business case or outline pain points addressed by the feature. Include any relevant user stories, scenarios, or use cases to illustrate its value.
  • Set Priority and Impact: Use Rhythmic’s priority settings to mark the feature as essential, important, or nice-to-have. If applicable, add projected impact details for users or workflows.

2. Move the Idea to Your Team’s Backlog

  • Transition to Jira: Once the idea is fleshed out and approved as a priority, create a feature request in Jira. Link the Rhythmic idea to the Jira ticket to maintain traceability.
  • Refine with Details: In Jira, expand on the feature with technical specifications or requirements as needed. Assign the ticket to the appropriate sprint or backlog based on your team’s planning process.

3. Communicate & Collaborate

  • Engage During Sprint Planning: Discuss the new feature during sprint planning or refinement sessions. Use this time to clarify requirements, gather feedback, and make adjustments.
  • Follow Up & Track Progress: Monitor the Jira ticket’s status. Rhythmic allows for automatic updates on the idea’s progression, so you’ll stay informed as development proceeds.

4. Stay Involved

  • Provide Additional Context: During development, be prepared to answer questions or provide further clarification as needed. This helps ensure that the feature aligns closely with your original vision.

This workflow ensures that your feature requests are organized, detailed, and actionable, leading to a smoother transition from idea generation in Rhythmic to implementation in Jira.
